# Steven Dux Trading Strategies - Comprehensive Research

## 1. CORE STRATEGY: Shorting Volatile Low-Float Stocks

Steven Dux's core strategy is **short-selling volatile, low-float penny stocks** listed on major exchanges (primarily Nasdaq). Despite being labeled a "penny stock trader," he explicitly **avoids OTC/Pink Sheet markets** due to insufficient liquidity to fill orders. He trades stocks priced between **$1.50 and $15 per share**.

His edge: He identifies pump-and-dump schemes and shorts when the promoter/scammer sells. He plays the exact opposite position of what retail crowds are trying to buy, profiting when the inevitable dump occurs.

**Key Philosophy:** "I looked at the stock market as a bunch of data and a couple of scenarios in which the stock can go up and down." He trades as robotically as possible, treating the market as a statistics problem rather than a prediction game.

He teaches **8 core strategies (4 Long + 4 Short)** plus bonus material on dip buying and combining strategies.

## 2. SPECIFIC PATTERNS HE TRADES

### SHORT PATTERNS (His Primary Focus):

#### a) Gap Up Short
- A stock gaps up significantly in pre-market and performs a specific pattern after market open
- Win percentage: **70-80%** (advanced strategy requiring pre-market chart reading)
- Certain patterns can only be used on 1st or 2nd green days
- First green day needs to gap up over 100%
- Profit range can be around -26% (stock drops 26%)

#### b) Bounce Short
- Waits patiently on the way up, then trades the bounce/reversal
- On swing positions going multi-day, covers about **75% of size** on first day
- If ticker trades with enough volume, there's typically a bounce the next day
- Keeps **25% of position** so he can add on the second day
- Uses resistance levels to identify entry points

#### c) First Red Day Short
- Looks for multi-day runners expected to have a pullback
- Waits for stock to make one final push upward but fail to break a resistance level
- Goes short when stock retests that resistance area
- Sets risk just above the resistance level
- Selling pressure from profit-taking creates panic-like effect

#### d) Overextended Gap Down
- Mentioned as a setup in the Duxinator course
- Trades stocks that have gapped down after being overextended

#### e) Double Day Intraday Top / Max Dollar Block
- An intraday pattern for identifying tops
- Uses dollar block calculations to estimate resistance levels
- Formula-based approach to calculating where stocks will stall

### LONG PATTERNS:

#### a) Multi-Day Breakout
- Stock breaks through 52-week high after extended consolidation
- The longer the consolidation period, the better the setup
- Breakouts categorized as: multi-week, multi-month, multi-year
- **Win rate: 60-70%**, average return: 30-100%
- Occurs approximately **30-50 times per year**
- Example: Bought 60,000 shares at $4.70, sold at $8.70 pre-market next morning = $300K profit

#### b) Panic Dip Buy
- Only buys dips on multi-day runners where buyers see the price drop as a discount
- NOT just any stock that dips -- must have context of a multi-day move
- Figured out the range of bouncing: "What's the maximum range of spiking? What's the average range of pulling back, so I can dip-buy?"

#### c) Reverse Short (Combination Strategy)
- After going long on a breakout, immediately shorts after the stock reaches new highs
- Effective about **26% of the time** as a standalone
- Works because traders want to take profits after new all-time highs
- Used as a follow-up to long positions

#### d) Industry News / Catalyst-Based Long
- Combines multi-day breakout with industry news catalysts
- Looks for stocks with legitimate sector-wide catalysts

## 4. RISK MANAGEMENT APPROACH

### Core Principles:
- **"We can't always control how much we make, but we can almost always control how much we lose."**
- Every trade must have a pre-identified point where the pattern has broken down and you must cut the loss
- Sizes positions based on the **winning percentage of each pattern** -- more capital to higher-probability setups
- Avoids overtrading by sticking to **A+ setups only**

### Pattern-Specific Stops:
- Each pattern has a **maximum stop level** (different for each pattern/category)
- If a pattern's win rate drops below acceptable levels, he stops trading that pattern entirely
- Tracks performance against maximum potential -- aims for **85% of max potential performance**

### Early Career Statistics:
- Average loss: **$0.08/share**
- Average winner: **$0.19/share** (2.4x larger than losers)
- Win rate: **71%**
- June 2020 example: 32 trades, 25 winners, 7 losers = **78% win rate**

### General Pattern Win Rates:
- Most strategies he follows have approximately a **65% win ratio**
- Some less common strategies work as much as **75% of the time**
- Some strategies work **55% of the time** (still profitable with proper R:R)

## 5. POSITION SIZING METHODOLOGY

### The 10% Float / 1% Volume Rule:
- **Never exceed 10% of the stock's float** in position size
- **Never exceed 1% of the daily volume**
- Going over 10% of float will affect the price against you
- Learned this the hard way: once took 15% of float, resulted in 40% losses trying to exit

### Volume-Based Sizing:
- Position size is calculated based on the ticker's volume, not a fixed dollar amount
- Different position sizes for different categories (lower float/lower cap = smaller size)
- Generally, position cannot exceed over 5% rule (for standard tickers)
- Most intraday low-float runners can only handle **100,000 to 600,000 share positions**

### Share Size Variation:
- Uses different share sizes based on pattern category and market cap
- Does NOT use the same size on every ticker
- Categorizes tickers into sections (e.g., lower float with lower market cap)
- Scales position sizing gradually as confidence in patterns grows

### Swing Position Management:
- On multi-day shorts: covers **75%** of position same day, holds **25%** for next day add
- On overnight longs: willing to hold for half a day to next pre-market

- Wakes up at **5:30 AM** every day to scan for trading opportunities
- Morning spent scanning with pre-market scanners for stocks fitting his criteria
- If no setups meet criteria, **does not force trades**
- After morning trading session, has breakfast, then mentors students/webinars

### Trading Sessions:
- **Pre-market:** Scans and identifies setups; occasionally enters positions pre-market
- **Market hours (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET):** Primary trading window; most shorts initiated after open
- **After hours / overnight:** Sometimes holds positions overnight (e.g., buying afternoon, selling pre-market next morning)
- Typically holds positions for **a few hours** to prevent overtrading
- Maximum hold time is usually **one day** (covers next day for shorts)

### Pre-Market Specific:
- Pre-market chart reading is essential for gap-up short strategy
- Identifies key resistance levels in pre-market (e.g., saw $7 as major resistance in pre-market for TNXP)
- Uses pre-market volume to estimate intraday volume at EOD (has a formula for this)

## 8. HOW HE IDENTIFIES STOCKS TO TRADE

### Scanning Criteria:
1. **Top percent gainers** -- Primary scan filter
2. **Price range:** $1.50 - $15 per share
3. **Market cap:** Under $200 million (sweet spot: $50M - $100M for some strategies, tracks up to $200M)
4. **Float:** Low float preferred (higher volatility)
5. **Volume:** Must have sufficient liquidity for position sizing
6. **Exchange:** Nasdaq only (avoids OTC, Pink Sheets, OTCBB)

### Market Cap Segregation Process:
- Different market caps behave differently
- Level 2 order books differ by cap size
- Volume patterns differ by cap size
- Trader psychology/reactions differ by cap size
- Segregates caps and tracks statistics separately for each category
- Looks for repeating patterns and similarities within each cap category

### Selection Decision:
- Only trades when stocks fit criteria based on his established strategies
- Borrows shares (for short selling) only after confirming the stock is tradable
- If no stocks meet criteria on a given day, does NOT trade
- Calculates risk based on percentage (not price) -- a key distinction

## 9. APPROACH TO CATALYSTS

### Pump-and-Dump Identification:
- Primary catalyst he exploits: **pump-and-dump schemes**
- Identifies when promoters accumulate shares, pump via social media (Twitter, StockTwits, Discord, Telegram, email), then dump
- Shorts when the scammer sells, profiting from the inevitable collapse
- SEC crackdowns moved pump activity from traditional channels to social media

### News / Major Events:
- Shorts after **major news events** that create unsustainable price spikes
- DWAC/DJT trade: Shorted after 400%+ spike on Trump social media merger news ($13M profit in 3 days)
- Combines news catalysts with pattern recognition (e.g., Multi-Day Breakout + Industry News for longs)

### Dilution / SEC Filings:
- **Shelf offerings (S-3/F-3 filings):** Allows companies to raise money within 3 years of registration
- These are typically **negative catalysts** for penny stocks
- Companies do secondary offerings when low on cash (common in penny stock land)
- Dilutive offerings reduce per-share value as market cap stays same with more shares outstanding
- Monitors SEC filings for these catalysts

### Volume as Catalyst Indicator:
- **Volume prediction:** Key component of strategy -- predicts future volume based on pre-market activity
- **Gap up short volume prediction:** Specific volume analysis for gap-up short setups
- **Fading volume prediction:** Identifies when volume is drying up (signal for reversal)
- **Volume comparison:** Compares current volume to historical patterns for the stock's market cap category

## 10. TECHNICAL INDICATORS / CHART PATTERNS

### What He Does NOT Use:
- **No Bollinger Bands**
- **No MACD**
- **No fancy technical indicators** -- believes they are "worse than useless" and "cloud your judgment"
- States that all patterns found free online "do not work"

### What He DOES Use:

#### Chart Patterns:
- **Support and resistance levels** -- Primary technical tool
- **Head and Shoulders** -- Used for short entries (e.g., shorted at second shoulder on TNXP)
- **Flags, wedges, breakouts, trend reversals** -- Identifies recurring price patterns
- **52-week high breakouts** -- For multi-day breakout long strategy
- **Consolidation patterns** -- Looks for prolonged consolidation before breakouts

#### Level 2 / Order Flow:
- **Level 2 data** -- Secondary strategy for reading market depth
- Uses Level 2 to track whether a stock has potential for significant movement
- Notes that Level 2 behavior differs by market cap category
- **Dollar block resistance** -- Calculates resistance using dollar block formulas

#### Volume Analysis:
- **Volume prediction models** -- Estimates EOD volume from pre-market volume
- **Volume gap theory** -- Significance in predicting market trends
- **Consolidation range / spiking percentage ratio** -- Minimum ratio required for shorting into consolidating resistance
- **Neutralized area identification** -- Zones where buying/selling pressure equalizes

#### Statistical / Data-Based:
- **Extension prediction based on market cap** -- How far a stock can extend based on its cap size
- **Historical pattern databases** -- Thousands of spreadsheets tracking pattern performance
- **Win rate tracking per pattern** -- Calculates exact win percentage before trading any pattern
- **Sample size requirement** -- Tracks at least one sample of a pattern before trading it
- Needs to know: frequency, reward potential, and risk for each pattern
